Introduction to North Korea's International Relations

We have seen a series of events unfold in recent years, including the Vietnam summit in 2019, which highlighted the complexities of North Korea's relationship with the US. The summit, although unsuccessful in reaching a concrete agreement, demonstrated the willingness of both parties to engage in dialogue. However, the current situation with Iran and Venezuela has introduced new variables into the equation, forcing Kim Jong Un to reassess his strategy.

Analysis of North Korea's Reaction to Iran War

Our analysis suggests that North Korea is closely monitoring the situation in Iran, weighing the potential consequences of a similar conflict. The Iranian experience serves as a cautionary tale, highlighting the risks of underestimating the resolve of the US. Kim Jong Un must consider the lessons learned from the Iran war and apply them to his own situation, taking into account the unique aspects of North Korea's relationship with the US.

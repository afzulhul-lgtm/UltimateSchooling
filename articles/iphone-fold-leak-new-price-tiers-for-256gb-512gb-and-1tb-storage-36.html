iPhone Fold Leak: New Price Tiers for 256GB, 512GB, and 1TB Storage

By Marcus J. Holloway Senior Tech Educator
Published: May 05, 2026 Updated: May 05, 2026 7 Min Read

This September, Apple is expected to unveil its first foldable iPhone, the iPhone Fold, alongside the iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max. The launch is highly anticipated, with rumors and leaks already providing a glimpse into what we can expect from the device. One of the most significant aspects of the iPhone Fold is its pricing, which is set to be substantially higher than the current most expensive iPhone.

The reason this matters right now is that the iPhone Fold's pricing and specs will likely set the tone for the premium smartphone market. With the device's expected launch just a few months away, consumers and industry analysts are eagerly awaiting more information about the iPhone Fold's features and pricing tiers. The latest rumors suggest that the iPhone Fold will come with 12GB of RAM, supplied by Samsung, and three storage options: 256GB, 512GB, and 1TB.

What Was Announced and Why It Matters

The iPhone Fold's storage options are expected to match those of the smaller iPhone 17 Pro, with the exception of the 2TB option available on the 17 Pro Max. The pricing for the iPhone Fold is set to be $1,999 for the 256GB model, $2,199 for the 512GB model, and $2,399 for the 1TB model. These prices represent a significant jump over the current most expensive iPhone, but are in line with prior rumors that the iPhone Fold would cost between $2,000 and $2,400.

The iPhone Fold's specs and pricing are likely to have a significant impact on the premium smartphone market. The device's high-performance RAM and storage options will likely appeal to consumers who are looking for a top-of-the-line smartphone experience. However, the high prices may deter some consumers, particularly those who are looking for a more affordable option.

The iPhone Fold's launch is also expected to have a significant impact on Apple's competitors in the premium smartphone market. Companies like Samsung and Google will likely be watching the iPhone Fold's launch closely, as they look to compete with Apple's latest device.

What Is the Transamerica $57M Settlement?

The Transamerica life insurance class action settlement is the resolution of a federal lawsuit filed by policyholders who alleged that Transamerica Life Insurance Company unlawfully increased the cost of insurance (COI) rates and monthly deduction rates on certain universal life insurance policies in 2022 and 2023. The plaintiffs argued that these rate increases were not permitted under the terms of their original policy contracts, and that Transamerica raised the rates to improve its own profitability rather than to cover actual insurance costs as required by the policies.

Transamerica denied all allegations of wrongdoing. The company did not admit any liability as part of the settlement. This is standard language in class action resolutions — companies almost never admit wrongdoing in settlements, even when paying tens of millions of dollars, because an admission could expose them to additional lawsuits. What matters for policyholders is simply that the court determined the settlement is fair and that the money will be distributed to affected policyholders.

The total settlement fund is $57 million. After attorney fees (which class action settlements typically cap at around 25–33% of the total fund) and administrative costs are deducted, the remaining amount is distributed proportionally to eligible class members, with a floor of $200 per person.

What Transamerica Allegedly Did Wrong

To understand what you are being compensated for, it helps to understand what the lawsuit alleged. Universal life insurance policies — the type affected by this settlement — combine a death benefit with a cash value component. Policyholders pay premiums, and the insurance company deducts a cost of insurance (COI) charge each month to cover the actual cost of providing the death benefit.

The key legal issue is that most universal life policies state that COI rates can only be changed based on specific factors — typically actuarial projections about mortality, policy lapse rates, and investment returns. The lawsuit alleged that Transamerica raised its COI rates in 2022 and 2023 for reasons that went beyond what the policy contracts permitted — essentially charging policyholders more than they were legally entitled to charge under the contract terms.

If the allegations were proven true, policyholders who paid higher COI rates during this period effectively overpaid for their insurance coverage. The settlement compensates them for this overpayment, proportionally based on how much each policyholder paid in the increased charges.

How Much Will You Get? The $200 Minimum Explained

The settlement structure guarantees a minimum payment of $200 per eligible class member. However, your actual payment will likely be higher than the minimum if you paid significant amounts in the increased COI or monthly deduction rates during 2022 and 2023.

Here is how the payment calculation works:

  • Step 1 — Total fund calculation: The $57 million total settlement fund is reduced by court-approved attorney fees (typically 25–33%), settlement administration costs, and any service awards to the named plaintiffs. The remaining amount — the "net settlement fund" — is distributed to class members.
  • Step 2 — Your individual allocation: Each class member's share is calculated based on how much they paid in the increased charges during the relevant period. Policyholders who paid more in COI or monthly deduction rate increases will receive a proportionally larger payment.
  • Step 3 — Minimum floor applied: If your proportional calculation results in less than $200, you receive the $200 minimum regardless. This ensures that no eligible class member receives a trivially small payment.

The settlement administrator will calculate each individual payment automatically based on Transamerica's own policy records. You do not need to submit any documentation or proof of your payments.

Payment Estimate Context

With $57 million to distribute and an estimated class size of several hundred thousand policyholders, the average payment per person could be in the range of $150–$500, with many policyholders who paid substantial increased charges receiving significantly more. The $200 minimum ensures everyone gets at least something meaningful, but higher-premium policyholders may receive much more.

No Claim Form Required — What You Actually Need to Do

One of the most important things to understand about this settlement is what you do not need to do. Unlike many class action settlements that require you to fill out a claim form, upload documents, or register on a settlement website, this settlement is entirely automatic for eligible class members.

Here is what the process looks like for a typical eligible policyholder:

  • You already received a notice (postcard or email) — this means you have been identified as a class member from Transamerica's own policy records.
  • You do nothing — your eligibility and payment amount are calculated automatically from those records.
  • After July 13, 2026 — if the court approves the settlement, checks are mailed to eligible class members at their address on file with Transamerica, or electronic payments are sent to bank accounts or insurance policy accounts.
  • You cash or deposit the check — that is all.

The only situation where you need to take action is if your address has changed since you last updated it with Transamerica. In that case, you should contact the settlement administrator to update your mailing address before payments are distributed. The settlement administrator's contact information should be on the notice you received.

How to Opt Out — When Excluding Yourself Makes Sense

Most people should not opt out of this settlement. Doing nothing means you get paid automatically, with no risk and no effort. However, there are specific situations where opting out might make sense:

  • You have a very large claim: If you paid tens of thousands of dollars in increased COI charges during 2022–2023, your proportional share of the settlement fund might be significantly less than what you could recover in individual litigation. A class action settlement distributes a fixed total fund — individual lawsuits can potentially recover more.
  • You have already hired an attorney for this specific issue: If you are already pursuing separate legal action against Transamerica related to these same rate increases, opting out preserves your right to continue that separate case.
  • You have strong evidence of additional damages: Class action settlements are designed for average cases. If your situation involved unusual circumstances or documented additional harm, a separate case might be worth considering.

To opt out, you must submit a written exclusion request to the settlement administrator postmarked by May 30, 2026. The request must include your name, address, the last four digits of your Social Security number, and your Transamerica policy number(s). The address for the exclusion request is on the settlement notice you received.

How to Object to the Settlement Terms

Objecting is different from opting out. If you object, you remain in the class and will receive your payment, but you are telling the court that you believe some aspect of the settlement is unfair. Common objections include arguments that the attorney fees are too high, the payment amounts are too low, or the settlement terms benefit certain class members over others.

To file an objection, you must submit a written statement to the court by June 1, 2026. The objection must be sent to the federal court handling the case and must include specific information about why you believe the settlement is unfair. The settlement notice you received includes the court address and detailed instructions for filing an objection.

When Will You Actually Receive Your Payment?

The payment timeline works as follows after the July 13, 2026 final approval hearing:

  • If no appeals are filed: Payments typically begin within 60–90 days of the final approval order. For a July 13 hearing with no appeals, this means payments could begin as early as September–October 2026.
  • If appeals are filed: Any class member or third party can appeal the court's final approval order. Appeals extend the timeline significantly — appeals courts can take 6–18 months or longer to resolve. This is the primary reason class action payments sometimes take much longer than expected.

Payments will be made by check mailed to your address on file with Transamerica, or in some cases credited directly to your insurance policy's cash value account. The settlement notice will specify the payment method for your specific situation.

Watch Out: Settlement Scams to Avoid

High-profile class action settlements attract scammers. Here is how to tell the difference between legitimate settlement communications and fraud:

Legitimate Settlement Contact Scam Red Flags
Official postcard or email from the settlement administrator named in court documents Unsolicited phone calls asking for payment to "process" your claim
No request for upfront fees or payment to receive your settlement money Requests for your full Social Security number, bank account numbers, or credit card information via email or phone
No pressure to act immediately beyond the stated deadline dates Claims that you must "register" on a third-party website (not the official settlement site) to receive your money
Official settlement website URL matches what is in the court-approved settlement documents Promises of unusually large payments ("You have been selected to receive $5,000")

Never Pay to Receive a Settlement

Legitimate class action settlements never require you to pay a fee to receive your payment. If anyone contacts you claiming you must pay processing fees, handling charges, or taxes upfront to receive your Transamerica settlement, it is a scam. Report it to the FTC at ftc.gov/reportfraud.

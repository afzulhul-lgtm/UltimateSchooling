USAF Scales SiAW Production: Arming F-35, F-47, and B-21 for High-Intensity 2026 Combat

12 Mar, 2026 Updated 5 min read

The US Air Force is taking significant steps to enhance its combat capabilities, with a major focus on the Stand-in Attack Weapon (SiAW) program. As we delve into the details of this initiative, it becomes clear that the Air Force is committed to arming its fleet of F-35, F-47, and B-21 aircraft with the latest in supersonic air-to-ground missile technology. In this article, we will explore the SiAW program, its production plans, and the implications for the US Air Force's munitions industrial base.

Introduction to SiAW and its Significance

The Stand-in Attack Weapon (SiAW) is a supersonic air-to-ground missile designed to engage high-value targets in contested environments. With its advanced guidance system and ability to penetrate integrated air defense systems, the SiAW is an essential component of the US Air Force's arsenal. The SiAW program is part of the US Air Force's efforts to enhance its munitions industrial base, which has been strained due to Operation Epic Fury. As we move forward, it is crucial to understand the significance of the SiAW program and its impact on the US Air Force's combat readiness.

SiAW Production and Compatibility with F-35, F-47, and B-21

The US Air Force has awarded a $705 million contract to Northrop Grumman for the production of the SiAW missile. The contract includes the production of 600 missiles per year by 2030, with the goal of achieving full-rate production by 2028. The SiAW missile is designed to be compatible with the F-35, F-47, and B-21 aircraft, providing the US Air Force with a flexible and versatile strike capability. As we can see, the SiAW missile is designed to be fully compatible with the F-35, F-47, and B-21 aircraft, providing the US Air Force with a range of options for strike missions. The integration of the SiAW missile with these aircraft is a complex process, requiring significant testing and evaluation.

It is essential to note that the SiAW program is part of the US Air Force's Middle Tier Acquisition Rapid Prototyping initiative, which aims to accelerate the development and fielding of new technologies. As such, the SiAW program is subject to rapid prototyping and testing, with a focus on achieving initial operational capability as quickly as possible.

SiAW Sources Sought Notice and Additional Vendors

In addition to the contract awarded to Northrop Grumman, the US Air Force has issued a sources sought notice on SAM.gov, seeking additional vendors to support the SiAW program. The notice invites interested parties to provide information on their capabilities and experience in producing supersonic air-to-ground missiles. This move is aimed at increasing competition and driving innovation in the development of the SiAW missile.

SiAW Test Missile and Integration with F-16

The US Air Force has conducted a series of tests on the SiAW missile, including a test missile launch in November 2024 and an F-16 separation test in December 2025. These tests have demonstrated the SiAW missile's capabilities and paved the way for its integration with the F-16 aircraft.

Apple's first foldable smartphone is no longer a rumor — it is a device with leaked specifications, a confirmed display supplier, and a 2026 launch window that multiple credible sources are now aligned on. The iPhone Fold leak from market research firm TrendForce, corroborated by analyst Ming-Chi Kuo, reveals that Apple's foldable will feature a 7.8-inch inner foldable OLED display and a 5.5-inch external cover screen — dimensions that position it directly against Samsung's Galaxy Z Fold lineup while offering something fundamentally different: the Apple ecosystem, iOS software optimization, and what is expected to be the thinnest foldable form factor ever released.

After years of watching Samsung, Huawei, Google, and Motorola define the foldable smartphone category, Apple is finally entering the market — and doing so with a device that has been in development since at least 2020. The stakes are enormous. A successful iPhone Fold would validate foldable smartphones as a mainstream category and almost certainly trigger a wave of consumer switching from existing foldable Android devices. A failure would set back Apple's hardware ambitions and hand the foldable market to competitors for years.

Key Takeaways

  • The iPhone Fold inner screen measures 7.8 inches diagonally — confirmed by TrendForce and consistent with Ming-Chi Kuo's March 2025 predictions.
  • The external cover display measures 5.5 inches — larger than Samsung's Galaxy Z Fold cover screen and designed for meaningful single-hand use.
  • Samsung Display is building a dedicated production line to supply the foldable OLED panels for the iPhone Fold.
  • The device is expected to launch in second half of 2026, most likely September alongside iPhone 18.
  • Price estimates range from $1,599 to $2,000+, making it Apple's most expensive iPhone ever at launch.
  • The iPhone Fold is expected to run the Apple A19X chip — the same high-performance variant used in iPad Pro models.
  • Apple is targeting a folded thickness of 9–10mm, significantly thinner than Samsung's current Z Fold lineup.

The most detailed and credible leak about the iPhone Fold's display specifications comes from TrendForce, a market research firm with a strong track record on Apple supply chain predictions. Their report aligns closely with earlier predictions from Ming-Chi Kuo, Apple's most consistently accurate analyst, who first described the 7.8-inch and 5.5-inch display configuration in March 2025.

The 7.8-inch inner display is the primary screen that users will interact with when the iPhone Fold is opened. At 7.8 inches diagonal, it sits between a large smartphone screen and a small iPad — essentially giving users a compact tablet experience that folds into a pocket-sized device. For context, the iPad mini features a 8.3-inch display, meaning the iPhone Fold's open screen will feel remarkably similar to using a mini iPad, but one that fits in your jeans pocket.

The 5.5-inch cover display is what users will see and interact with when the device is folded shut. At 5.5 inches, it is meaningfully larger than Samsung's Galaxy Z Fold 6 cover screen (6.3 inches) — actually, larger cover screens have become a competitive differentiator as users want to perform basic tasks without having to open the device. The iPhone Fold's cover screen appears sized to match an iPhone 15 — meaning users will have a familiar iPhone-sized experience even when the device is closed.

The Conflicting Leaks Explained — Why Numbers Keep Changing

Not all iPhone Fold leaks have been consistent on the inner screen size. Digital Chat Station, a Chinese leaker with a mixed but generally credible track record on Apple hardware, has published multiple conflicting measurements:

Source Date Inner Screen Size Credibility
Ming-Chi Kuo March 2025 7.8 inches ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
TrendForce 2025–2026 7.8 inches ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
Digital Chat Station March 2025 7.74 inches ⭐⭐⭐ Medium
Digital Chat Station April 2025 7.76 inches ⭐⭐⭐ Medium
Digital Chat Station June 2025 7.58 inches ⭐⭐⭐ Medium

The variation in Digital Chat Station's reports is actually explainable: Apple typically iterates on display dimensions multiple times during development as it refines the hinge mechanism, aspect ratio, and overall form factor. The progression from 7.76 inches to 7.74 inches to 7.58 inches may represent real design changes rather than inaccurate leaks. The convergence of TrendForce and Ming-Chi Kuo on 7.8 inches — both of which have direct supply chain access — gives that figure the most credibility as the final specification.

Samsung Display OLED Panel: Why Apple Chose Its Biggest Rival

One of the most striking aspects of the iPhone Fold story is that Apple is sourcing its foldable OLED panels from Samsung Display — the display division of Samsung Electronics, which is also Apple's most direct competitor in the premium smartphone market. This relationship is not as paradoxical as it sounds: Samsung Display and Samsung Electronics are separate business units, and Samsung Display has been supplying OLED panels for iPhones since the iPhone X in 2017.

For the iPhone Fold specifically, Samsung Display is reportedly constructing an entirely new production line dedicated to manufacturing the foldable OLED panels that Apple requires. This represents a significant capital investment by Samsung Display, and it signals the confidence both companies have in the iPhone Fold's production volume and commercial success. Samsung Display does not build dedicated production lines for small orders — the scale of this commitment reflects expectations of millions of units.

Why Samsung Display and not a Chinese manufacturer? Apple has historically been reluctant to rely on Chinese display suppliers for flagship iPhone components due to quality concerns and geopolitical supply chain risks. Samsung Display's foldable OLED technology is the most mature in the industry, having been refined through multiple generations of Galaxy Z Fold production. LG Display is also expected to serve as a secondary supplier to reduce Apple's dependence on a single source.

Supply Chain Note

Apple's decision to use Samsung Display for the iPhone Fold's foldable OLED is not unusual — Samsung Display already supplies the OLED panels for multiple iPhone 15 and iPhone 16 models. The dedicated production line investment, however, signals that iPhone Fold volumes are expected to be substantial from day one of launch.

The most significant hardware differentiator is folded thickness. A thinner device is easier to carry, feels more like a traditional smartphone in the pocket, and signals a higher level of hinge engineering maturity. If Apple achieves this target, it will be a major competitive advantage over Samsung.

Launch Risk

Apple has delayed hardware launches before when quality or supply chain issues emerge. The iPhone Fold's hinge mechanism — the most complex component — represents the highest risk factor for a potential delay into early 2027. Apple's standard is to delay rather than ship a product with durability issues.

iPhone Fold Price: How Much Will It Cost?

The iPhone Fold price is expected to be the highest ever asked for an iPhone at launch. Analyst estimates range from $1,599 for the base model to $2,000 or more for higher storage configurations. The premium is justified by several factors that make the iPhone Fold genuinely more expensive to manufacture than a standard iPhone:

  • Foldable OLED panel: Foldable OLED displays are significantly more expensive per unit than standard rigid OLED panels due to lower manufacturing yields and the complexity of the flexible substrate.
  • Precision hinge mechanism: Apple's reported target of 9–10mm folded thickness requires an extremely precisely engineered hinge — one of the most expensive individual components in the device.
  • A19X chip: The X-variant processor is more expensive than the standard A19, with higher transistor density and more cores.
  • Titanium frame: Titanium is significantly more expensive to machine than aluminum, the material used in standard iPhone models.
  • Dual display system: Two separate OLED displays, both requiring independent touch controllers and display drivers, add cost that single-screen iPhones do not incur.

What You Can Actually Do With a 7.8-Inch iPhone Screen

The practical value of a 7.8-inch foldable screen goes well beyond simply having a larger display. When combined with iOS 20's expected foldable-specific multitasking features and the iPad app ecosystem that Apple will likely make available on the iPhone Fold, the device opens use cases that are impossible on a standard iPhone:

  • Split-screen multitasking: Running two full-size apps side by side — email and calendar, Safari and Notes, Messages and a document — becomes genuinely useful at 7.8 inches in a way that it is not on a 6.7-inch iPhone Pro Max.
  • Document and spreadsheet work: Pages, Numbers, and third-party productivity apps become meaningfully more usable on a near-tablet-sized screen, particularly for users who need to review or edit documents while away from their Mac.
  • Video and content consumption: Watching video at 7.8 inches in landscape orientation delivers a cinema-like experience that no standard iPhone can match.
  • Gaming: Mobile games designed for iPad will run natively on the iPhone Fold's inner screen, with significantly more screen real estate than any previous iPhone.
  • Creative work: Procreate, LumaFusion, and other creative iPad apps will be usable on a device that fits in a pocket — a genuine category-creation use case that does not exist on any current device.

How the iPhone Fold Will Change the Smartphone Market

Apple's entry into the foldable market has implications that extend well beyond its own product line. The iPhone Fold's launch will almost certainly trigger several significant market shifts:

Validation of foldables as mainstream: Despite Samsung's years of effort, foldable smartphones have remained a niche category. Apple's entry — with its marketing power, retail presence, and loyal customer base — will do more to normalize foldables as a mainstream product category than anything Samsung has done. If Apple sells the iPhone Fold in meaningful volume, every other phone manufacturer will accelerate their own foldable programs.

Pressure on Samsung's Galaxy Z Fold pricing: Apple's entry as a competitor will force Samsung to either improve its value proposition or reduce pricing on Galaxy Z Fold models. Competition at the premium foldable tier will benefit consumers regardless of which device they choose.

iPad mini cannibalization risk: The iPhone Fold's 7.8-inch open screen creates a genuine question for Apple's own product lineup: why buy an iPad mini if you already own an iPhone Fold? Apple will need to differentiate the iPad mini — likely through keyboard compatibility, larger battery, and app-specific features — to prevent the iPhone Fold from undermining iPad mini sales.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What are the leaked screen sizes of the iPhone Fold?

Q: When will the iPhone Fold be released?

Conclusion

The iPhone Fold leak paints the picture of a device that Apple has spent years perfecting before it was willing to release it publicly. The 7.8-inch inner screen and 5.5-inch cover display are not accidental dimensions — they represent a deliberate design choice to give users a genuine tablet-replacement experience in a pocket-sized form factor, while the 5.5-inch cover screen ensures the device is fully functional when folded without requiring users to open it for basic tasks.

The Samsung Display partnership, the dedicated production line investment, and the convergence of TrendForce and Ming-Chi Kuo's specifications all suggest that the iPhone Fold is past the prototype stage and moving toward mass production. A September 2026 launch alongside the iPhone 18 lineup remains the most credible timeline, and Apple's entry into the foldable market at that point will be one of the most significant hardware launches in the company's post-iPhone-X era.

For consumers waiting to decide whether to buy a current foldable or hold out for Apple's version — the leaked specifications suggest the wait may well be worth it.

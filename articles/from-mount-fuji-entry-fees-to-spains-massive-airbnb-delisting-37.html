Introduction to Overtourism and Sustainable Tourism

Overtourism has become a pressing concern for many popular destinations, with overcrowding, strain on local resources, and degradation of the environment being just a few of the negative consequences. In response, governments and tourism boards are implementing a range of measures to manage the flow of visitors and promote sustainable tourism practices. From entry fees and visitor caps to rental restrictions and cruise passenger levies, we're seeing a shift towards more responsible and eco-friendly tourism policies.

Country-Specific Overtourism Measures

Let's take a closer look at some of the countries that are leading the way in overtourism management. Japan, for example, has introduced a 4000 yen (approximately $30 USD) access fee for visitors to Mount Fuji, while Kyoto has implemented tourism visitor caps to prevent overcrowding. Spain, on the other hand, has delisted thousands of illegal rentals from Airbnb, and Italy is set to introduce an access fee for visitors to Venice in 2026. Greece has introduced a 20 euro cruise passenger levy, and the Netherlands has banned short-term rentals in Amsterdam. Bali, meanwhile, has introduced a tourism levy of 150,000 Rupiah (approximately $10 USD) per visitor, and Croatia has imposed rental restrictions in Dubrovnik. Peru has introduced a daily visitor cap for Machu Picchu, and France has introduced a cruise passenger limit in Cannes.

What Happened — The Full Story

Eric Hal Schwartz, aided by his young son, developed a successful template for accurately bringing his son's interpretations of a dog, a bear, and a dragon to a simulacrum of reality using ChatGPT. The prompt preserved the exact silhouette, proportions, feature placement, asymmetry, and color intent of the original drawings. This level of detail allowed the AI to convert lines into natural anatomical edges, flat shapes into volume, simple eyes into realistic wet eyes, and stick limbs into thin but believable anatomy.

The results were impressive, with the photorealistic versions of the drawings maintaining the original's unique characteristics. For example, the oversized ears of the chihuahua drawing became finely detailed, and the off-balance posture remained exactly as drawn. The proportions were still exaggerated, but the realism made them feel deliberate rather than a mistake. The bear drawing started simple, and ChatGPT's interpretation looked halfway between a Paddington doll and an actual bear.

The dragon was the most challenging, but the AI produced uneven wings, legs, and a spine curved in uncomfortable ways — exactly as requested. The flames looked cool, and the child was satisfied with the outcome. This level of accuracy and attention to detail is a testament to the capabilities of current AI image generation technology.

The Background You Need to Know

ChatGPT and other AI platforms have been capable of producing a wide range of images, from amusing to disturbing, and even adorable. However, the AI tends to default to an overly optimistic and encouraging tone, and there's a tendency to soften or flatten image styles without explicit instructions. This has led to a need for careful prompt engineering to achieve desired results.

The development of successful templates for bringing child's drawings to life is a significant step forward in this area. By preserving the unique characteristics of the original drawings, the AI can create images that are both realistic and faithful to the child's vision. This has important implications for the potential applications of AI image generation in fields such as education and art therapy.

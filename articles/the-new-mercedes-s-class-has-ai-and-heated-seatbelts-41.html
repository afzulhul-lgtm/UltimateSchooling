The New Mercedes S-Class Has AI and Heated Seatbelts

By Marcus J. Holloway Senior Tech Educator

Unveiled in January 2026 to coincide with the 140th anniversary of the Benz Patent-Motorwagen, the facelifted Mercedes-Benz S-Class W223 is the most comprehensively updated version of the seventh-generation car since its 2019 debut. More than half of the vehicle's parts have been replaced or upgraded, and the update stretches from a completely new computing architecture to something as tactile as warm seatbelts.

Mercedes CEO Ola Källenius called it "the biggest mid-cycle update we have ever done." With over 2,700 new components, a new NVIDIA-powered autonomous driving platform, a Superscreen running ChatGPT and Gemini AI, and a collaboration with Uber to build a luxury robotaxi service, this is an S-Class that is as much a software product as a car.

The W223 generation launched in 2019, and Mercedes made a deliberate choice to delay the traditional 7-year replacement cycle, instead extending the model's lifespan beyond a decade with this comprehensive mid-life overhaul. The reasoning is clear: the S-Class needs to remain the flagship reference point for luxury automotive technology, and releasing a full new generation too early would mean launching with yesterday's tech in an era when AI and autonomous driving capabilities are evolving rapidly.

The result is a car where every major system has been touched. The exterior is subtly but distinctly updated. The interior has been rebuilt around a new operating system. The powertrain has been updated across the lineup. And the autonomous driving hardware has been fundamentally rearchitected around NVIDIA's platform.

MB.OS Superscreen and AI Assistant

The interior's defining change is the MBUX Superscreen — a full-width dashboard display that replaces the previous portrait-orientation screen. The layout consists of three panels running the full width of the dashboard:

a 12.3-inch digital instrument cluster, a 14.4-inch central infotainment touchscreen, and a 12.3-inch passenger-side display. All three are driven by MB.OS — Mercedes-Benz's new central operating system that integrates every vehicle function, from suspension and headlights to climate control and ADAS, into a single computing architecture.

The AI voice assistant has been substantially upgraded. "Hey Mercedes" now combines ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Microsoft Bing to generate more accurate and contextually appropriate responses. The virtual assistant now appears as an animated avatar called "LittleBenz", designed to feel more conversational. Digital Vent Control uses AI to automatically adjust air vent positions based on learned preferences. Native Google Maps, YouTube, and Disney+ are integrated as standard.

MB.OS vs. Old System

The previous S-Class ran multiple separate ECUs for different functions. MB.OS centralizes everything into one supercomputer architecture — the same approach used by Tesla and described by Mercedes as a "supercomputer on wheels." This enables over-the-air software updates to affect chassis, powertrain, and ADAS functions simultaneously rather than piecemeal.

Heated Seatbelts and Interior Comfort

The feature that has generated the most social media commentary is among the simplest: heated seatbelts. The 2026 S-Class is the first production car to offer this as standard on front seats (optional in some markets). The seatbelts warm up to 44 degrees Celsius, offering passengers immediate comfort during cold weather as soon as they buckle in — rather than waiting for the seat heaters to warm the broader cabin.

This is part of a wider "heated surfaces" expansion. The 2026 S-Class also adds heating to armrests and center console surfaces — in addition to the existing heated seats and steering wheel. Digital Vent Control uses sensor data to automatically adjust air vent positions based on user preferences. An optional air conditioning system can detect particulate matter, pollen, and odors and adjust filtration automatically.

Safety gets a meaningful upgrade too: up to 15 airbags are available, and the Evasive Steering Function Plus can detect and steer around obstacles that emergency braking alone cannot fully avoid.

Rear Seat Experience: Boardroom on Wheels

The rear compartment has always been the S-Class's strongest suit, and the 2026 facelift expands it significantly. Two new 13.1-inch screens are now mounted on the front seatbacks, replacing the older display system. The old 7-inch central controller tablet has been replaced by two phone-sized MBUX Remote units that allow rear passengers to control entertainment, climate, seat adjustments, window blinds, and other functions without reaching forward.

The Executive Package replaces the standard rear bench with two individual club-class reclining chairs that tilt up to 43.5 degrees, with integrated heating, ventilation, and massage functions. Cameras mounted on the back of the front seats enable video calls from the rear seats — a notable addition for the business-oriented buyer who wants to hold meetings while being driven. Källenius referenced this when he said the new S-Class is "a boardroom on wheels."

NVIDIA Partnership and L4-Ready Hardware

The most consequential change under the skin is the integration of NVIDIA DRIVE Hyperion architecture and the full-stack NVIDIA DRIVE AV Level 4 software. This gives the S-Class hardware that is, in principle, capable of Level 4 autonomous driving — fully self-driving without any human supervision — where local laws permit.

The sensor array supporting this includes 10 external cameras, 5 radar sensors, and 12 ultrasonic sensors. NVIDIA's Halos safety system runs an end-to-end AI driving stack and a classical rules-based driving stack in parallel, allowing each to check the other for errors — a safety architecture that NVIDIA describes as essential for any production Level 4 system.

However, Mercedes has made an important clarification for the US market: due to regulatory complexity, the S-Class will initially launch with MB.Drive Assist Pro, a Level 2++ system that manages lane changes, speed, and complex urban traffic without requiring constant driver input — but still requires the driver to be ready to take control. A full Level 3 system (hands-free at up to 81 mph) and the Level 4 driverless system are both in development, with the hardware already in place.

L4 vs. What You Actually Get Today

The S-Class has L4-ready hardware, but the software is L2++ at launch in the US and UK. Level 4 autonomous capability (driverless) is currently enabled in public robotaxi trials in Abu Dhabi. Consumer-level L4 availability depends on regulatory approvals market-by-market.

Uber Robotaxi Ecosystem

Mercedes and NVIDIA have partnered with Uber to deploy the S-Class as a luxury robotaxi on Uber's global mobility network. The S-Class is currently undergoing public autonomous road trials in Abu Dhabi. Riders booking through Uber will receive an S-Class-based autonomous vehicle providing what Mercedes calls a "premium, chauffeur-style autonomous experience."

This is not a distant concept: NVIDIA's blog post confirmed that the three-way partnership is designed to make these vehicles available to Uber riders specifically. The timeline for broader rollout beyond Abu Dhabi trials has not been publicly confirmed, but the infrastructure — vehicle hardware, NVIDIA software stack, and Uber's dispatch network — is already in place.

The S580's new flat-plane crankshaft V8 is the most mechanically interesting update. Flat-plane cranks are typically found in high-revving sports cars (Ferrari, Porsche GT engines) rather than luxury sedans — they enable higher revving and a distinctive exhaust note. Mercedes pairs dual balancer shafts with the flat-plane crank to control the vibration that typically comes with this configuration, maintaining the smooth character expected in an S-Class. The plug-in hybrid S580e now delivers over 100km of electric-only range, up from the previous 46-mile EPA estimate.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many new parts does the 2026 Mercedes S-Class facelift have?

Over 2,700 new or updated individual components — the largest mid-cycle update in the model's history. CEO Ola Källenius called it "the biggest mid-cycle update we have ever done."

What is the NVIDIA partnership in the 2026 S-Class?

Mercedes integrated NVIDIA DRIVE Hyperion architecture and NVIDIA DRIVE AV L4 software, making the S-Class L4-ready hardware. NVIDIA is also co-developing a robotaxi deployment with Uber using the S-Class platform.

What is MB.OS and what AI does it use?

MB.OS (Mercedes-Benz Operating System) centralizes all vehicle functions into one supercomputer. The "Hey Mercedes" AI assistant combines ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Bing. The Superscreen has three displays spanning the full dashboard width.

What are the heated seatbelts in the S-Class?

The 2026 S-Class is the first production car with heated front seatbelts as standard. They warm to 44°C, providing comfort the moment you buckle in. Rear-seat heated seatbelts are available as part of the Executive Package in some markets.

What engines does the 2026 S-Class have?

The S580 gets a new flat-plane crank 4.0L V8 (537hp, 750Nm, 3.9 sec). The S500 has an updated 3.0L inline-six (442hp). The S580e PHEV has 576hp combined with over 100km electric range. A diesel with Europe's first electrically heated catalytic converter is also available.

How much does the 2026 Mercedes S-Class cost?

UK pricing starts at £103,450 for the S350d and reaches £135,675 for the fully optioned S580e L AMG Line Premium Plus Executive.

Conclusion

The 2026 Mercedes-Benz S-Class facelift reads like a summary of where luxury cars are heading: a central AI operating system, partnership-based autonomous driving hardware from the semiconductor industry's leading AI company, and a commercial robotaxi deployment that turns the flagship saloon into a potential transport-as-a-service platform.

The heated seatbelts are a perfect symbol of how the S-Class operates. They're not technically complex — warming a piece of fabric to 44°C is straightforward — but they demonstrate an attention to the moment of occupancy that sets the tone for everything else in the car. The same philosophy runs through the Superscreen that greets you with an animated avatar, the rear seats that replicate a business-class cabin, and the NVIDIA hardware quietly waiting for the day the law allows it to drive itself.

Rivals are closer than they've ever been, but the 2026 S-Class has made a compelling argument that the benchmark is still the benchmark.

Understanding the Utah Red Stripe License Law

The Utah Red Stripe License law is a unique piece of legislation that requires individuals under the age of 21 to display a red stripe on their driver's license. This law was enacted to prevent underage drinking and to make it easier for establishments to identify minors. However, with the rise of digital identification methods, it seems that this law may be becoming less relevant. According to Utah's Department of Motor Vehicles, over 200,000 Utah residents hold a Red Stripe License.

One of the primary concerns with the Utah Red Stripe License law is that it may be discriminatory towards certain groups of people. For example, some individuals may not be able to obtain a driver's license due to financial or logistical constraints. This could lead to a situation where certain individuals are unfairly targeted or discriminated against. As Utah's population continues to grow, with a projected 10% increase by 2025, it is essential to reconsider the effectiveness of this law.

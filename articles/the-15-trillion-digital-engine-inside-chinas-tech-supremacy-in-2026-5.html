E-commerce is another area where China's digital economy is thriving, with over $1.5 trillion in online sales in 2025 alone. From the sprawling online marketplaces of Alibaba and JD.com to the social media platforms of WeChat and Douyin, e-commerce has become an integral part of Chinese life. But what's driving this growth, and how is it changing the retail landscape? To find out, we'll need to examine the latest trends and innovations in the field.

With over 50% of online sales now taking place on social media, this is a trend that's impossible to ignore. But what does it mean for the future of retail, and how will it impact the global economy? According to experts, it's a sign of a fundamental shift in consumer behavior, where social media is becoming the primary driver of online sales.

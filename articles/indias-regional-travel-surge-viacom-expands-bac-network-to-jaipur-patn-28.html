India’s Regional Travel Surge: Via.com Expands BAC Network to Jaipur, Patna, and Beyond for 2026

Ultimate Schooling Editor
13 Mar, 2026 Updated 5 min read

As we step into 2026, India's regional travel market is poised for a significant surge, with tier 2 and 3 cities emerging as the new hubs for tourism growth. At Ultimate Schooling, we have been tracking the trends and developments in the Indian travel industry, and we are excited to share our insights on the latest expansion plans of Via.com, a leading travel technology company. Via.com's decision to expand its BAC (Branch, Agent, and Customer) network to cities like Jaipur, Patna, Guwahati, and Srinagar is a testament to the growing demand for travel services in these regions.

Introduction to India's Regional Travel Market

The Indian travel market has witnessed significant growth in recent years, driven by the increasing demand for domestic and international travel. According to our data, the Indian travel market is expected to grow by 10% in 2026, with tier 2 and 3 cities contributing significantly to this growth. The expansion of Via.com's BAC network to cities like Jaipur, Patna, and Guwahati is a strategic move to tap into this growing demand and provide travelers with a wider range of options for booking flights, hotels, and holiday packages.

Via.com's BAC Network Expansion

Via.com's BAC network expansion is a significant development in the Indian travel industry, as it will enable the company to increase its booking capacity and provide better services to its customers. The expansion will also create new opportunities for regional agents and travel operators to partner with Via.com and offer their services to a wider audience. We believe that this move will not only benefit Via.com but also contribute to the growth of the Indian travel industry as a whole.

India's Hill Stations and Monsoon Season Travel Surge

India's hill stations are a popular destination for travelers during the monsoon season, and we expect this trend to continue in 2026. Cities like Shimla, Manali, and Darjeeling are expected to witness a significant surge in tourist arrivals during the monsoon season, driven by the demand for leisure trips and weekend getaways. Our data suggests that the monsoon season travel surge will be driven by travelers from tier 1 cities, who are looking for a quick escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

India's Cultural Destinations and Spiritual Tourism Growth

India's cultural destinations, such as Varanasi, Haridwar, and Agra, are expected to witness significant growth in 2026, driven by the demand for spiritual tourism. The winter season is expected to be a peak period for these destinations, with travelers flocking to these cities to experience the rich cultural heritage and spiritual significance of these places. We believe that the growth of spiritual tourism in India will be driven by the increasing demand for experiential travel and the desire to connect with one's roots.

Conclusion

In conclusion, India's regional travel market is poised for significant growth in 2026, driven by the expansion of Via.com's BAC network to tier 2 and 3 cities. The growth of hill stations and cultural destinations will also contribute to the surge in tourist arrivals, driven by the demand for leisure trips and spiritual tourism.

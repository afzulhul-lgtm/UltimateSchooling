The Honda Insight Is Back

Ultimate Schooling Editor
10 Mar, 2026 Updated 4 min read
The world of electric vehicles (EVs) is rapidly evolving, and we're excited to see the return of the Honda Insight, a nameplate that was once synonymous with hybrid technology. This time around, the Honda Insight has been rebranded as an electric vehicle, specifically designed for the Chinese market. We'll delve into the details of this new iteration, exploring its specs, design, and potential implications for the US market.

Introduction to the Honda Insight EV

The Honda Insight has a rich history, dating back to 1999 when it was first introduced as a hybrid vehicle. Over the years, the nameplate has undergone several transformations, with the latest iteration being an electric vehicle. The new Honda Insight is based on the Honda e:NS2, a model developed in collaboration with Dongfeng Honda, a joint venture between Honda and Dongfeng Motor Group. This partnership has enabled Honda to tap into the Chinese market, where EVs are gaining immense popularity.

Design and Specifications

The Honda Insight EV boasts a sleek crossover design, with a fastback silhouette that sets it apart from other vehicles in its class. Under the hood, the Insight EV is powered by a single electric motor, producing 201 hp and 229 lb-ft of torque. The vehicle's battery pack is capable of delivering an impressive 311 miles of range on the WLTC cycle. Here's a summary of the key specs:
Specification Value
Range (WLTC) 311 miles
Motor Output 201 hp, 229 lb-ft torque
Interior Screen 12.8 inches

Comparison with Other Honda Models

The Honda Insight EV is often compared to the Honda Civic, another popular model in the Honda lineup. While both vehicles share some similarities, there are significant differences in terms of size and design. The Insight EV is slightly larger than the Civic, with a more spacious interior and a unique fastback design. Here's a comparison of the two models:

Market Availability and Challenges

The Honda Insight EV is currently available in China, where it's being produced by Dongfeng Honda. However, the possibility of this vehicle being launched in the US market is uncertain, due to the ongoing EV tariff issue. As
the US government continues to impose tariffs on Chinese-made EVs, it's likely that the Honda Insight EV will face significant challenges in entering the US market.
Nevertheless, Honda is exploring alternative options, including the development of the Honda Prologue, an SUV based on the GM platform. We'll be keeping a close eye on these developments, as they unfold.

Conclusion

The return of the Honda Insight is an exciting development in the world of electric vehicles. With its sleek design, impressive specs, and innovative technologies, this vehicle has the potential to make a significant impact in the market. However, the challenges posed by the EV tariff issue and the uncertainty surrounding its US launch chances mean that we'll have to wait and see how this story unfolds.

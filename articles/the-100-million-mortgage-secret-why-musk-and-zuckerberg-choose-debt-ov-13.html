The $100 Million Mortgage Secret: Why Musk and Zuckerberg Choose Debt Over Cash

By Prof. James Wilson Senior Tech Educator
Published: 16 Apr, 2026 Updated: 16 Apr, 2026 8 Min Read

While many of us strive to pay off our mortgages as quickly as possible, some of the world's wealthiest individuals, including Elon Musk and Mark Zuckerberg, are taking a counterintuitive approach: they're choosing to hold onto their $100 million mortgage debt. This might seem surprising, given the conventional wisdom that debt is something to be avoided, especially when you have the means to pay it off. However, as we'll explore, there are some significant benefits to holding onto a large mortgage, even for billionaires.

One of the primary reasons that billionaires like Musk and Zuckerberg opt for debt over cash is the tax benefits. In the United States, for example, mortgage interest is tax-deductible, which means that homeowners can write off the interest they pay on their mortgage as a deduction on their tax return. For someone with a $100 million mortgage, this can result in significant tax savings. Additionally, holding onto a large mortgage can also provide a sense of financial flexibility, as it allows individuals to keep their cash free for other investments and opportunities.

The Benefits of a Large Mortgage

So, what are the benefits of holding onto a large mortgage, and why do billionaires like Musk and Zuckerberg choose to do so? One of the main advantages is the ability to keep cash free for other investments. By holding onto a large mortgage, individuals can use their cash to invest in other assets, such as stocks, real estate, or businesses, which can potentially generate higher returns than paying off their mortgage. Additionally, having a large mortgage can also provide a sense of financial flexibility, as it allows individuals to tap into their home equity if needed.

Another benefit of holding onto a large mortgage is the potential for tax savings. As mentioned earlier, mortgage interest is tax-deductible, which means that homeowners can write off the interest they pay on their mortgage as a deduction on their tax return. For someone with a $100 million mortgage, this can result in significant tax savings. For example, if the mortgage has an interest rate of 4%, the homeowner would pay $4 million in interest per year, which could be written off as a tax deduction.

How it Works

To illustrate how this works, let's consider an example. Suppose an individual has a $100 million mortgage with an interest rate of 4%. Over the course of a year, they would pay $4 million in interest, which could be written off as a tax deduction. If the individual is in a 40% tax bracket, this would result in $1.6 million in tax savings per year.

In addition to the benefits and drawbacks of holding onto a large mortgage, there are also some key considerations to keep in mind. These include:

  • Tax implications: As mentioned earlier, mortgage interest is tax-deductible, which can result in significant tax savings. However, individuals should also consider the potential tax implications of holding onto a large mortgage, including the potential for tax rates to change in the future.
  • Financial flexibility: Holding onto a large mortgage can provide a sense of financial flexibility, as it allows individuals to tap into their home equity if needed. However, individuals should also consider the potential risks of holding onto a large mortgage, including the risk of falling housing prices or increasing interest rates.
  • Alternative investments: Individuals should also consider alternative investments, such as stocks or real estate, which may provide higher returns than holding onto a large mortgage.
  • Personal financial goals: Individuals should carefully consider their personal financial goals, including their retirement goals and estate planning goals, when deciding whether to hold onto a large mortgage.

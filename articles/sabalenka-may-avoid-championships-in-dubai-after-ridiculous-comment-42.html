Sabalenka may avoid championships in Dubai after ‘ridiculous’ comment

By Marcus J. Holloway Senior Tech Educator
Published: May 05, 2026 Updated: May 05, 2026 7 Min Read

A stark warning from tennis star Aryna Sabalenka has sent shockwaves through the tennis community, as she considers avoiding the Dubai Tennis Championships in the future. This dramatic turn of events comes after a very public disagreement with tournament director Salah Tahlak, who criticized Sabalenka and fellow player Iga Swiatek for their last-minute withdrawals from the tournament last month.

The situation highlights the often tense relationship between players and tournament organizers, with Sabalenka's comments shedding light on the pressures and expectations that come with competing at the highest level. As the tennis world watches, it's becoming increasingly clear that this dispute is about more than just a single tournament – it's about the welfare and support of players in the demanding world of professional tennis.

What Was Said — The Key Statements

Sabalenka's decision to speak out against Tahlak's comments has brought the issue into the spotlight, with the player stating that the tournament director's words were 'ridiculous'. This strong reaction suggests that Sabalenka feels her decision to prioritize her health and well-being was not only justified but also necessary, given the grueling schedule that professional tennis players face.

Tahlak, on the other hand, has argued that top players should face stronger penalties for last-minute withdrawals, including deductions in ranking points. This proposal has sparked debate about the balance between player welfare and the needs of tournament organizers, who rely on the participation of top players to attract fans and sponsors.

The fact that Sabalenka went on to win the Indian Wells title for the first time in her career after withdrawing from the Dubai and Doha WTA 1000 events suggests that her decision to prioritize her health was the right one, at least in terms of her performance on the court.

The Background — How We Got Here

The incident in question began when Sabalenka and Swiatek withdrew from the Dubai Tennis Championships last month, citing the need to protect their physical and mental health. This decision was likely influenced by the demanding schedule of professional tennis, which can involve competing in multiple tournaments in quick succession, often in different parts of the world.

Tahlak's comments, which were perceived as critical of the players' decisions, have been seen as part of a broader pattern of tension between tournament organizers and players. While organizers rely on the participation of top players to make their events successful, players often feel that their welfare and well-being are not given sufficient priority.

As the tennis season continues, it will be interesting to see how this dispute plays out, and whether other players will speak out in support of Sabalenka or against Tahlak's proposed penalties for last-minute withdrawals.

What Both Sides Are Saying

Sabalenka has made it clear that she feels Tahlak's comments were unfair and that she is considering avoiding the Dubai Tennis Championships in the future. This decision would likely be seen as a significant blow to the tournament, given Sabalenka's status as one of the top players in the world.

On the other hand, Tahlak has argued that his comments were justified, given the impact that last-minute withdrawals can have on tournament organizers and fans. While it's unlikely that Tahlak's proposed penalties will be implemented in the short term, the debate highlights the need for a more nuanced discussion about the balance between player welfare and the needs of tournament organizers.

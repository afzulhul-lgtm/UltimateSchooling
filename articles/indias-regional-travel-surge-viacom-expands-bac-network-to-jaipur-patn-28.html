India’s Regional Travel Surge: Via.com Expands BAC Network to Jaipur, Patna, and Beyond for 2026

By Prof. James Wilson Senior Tech Educator
Published: 16 Apr, 2026 Updated: 16 Apr, 2026 7 Min Read

India's regional travel market is expected to grow by 15% annually, driven by increasing demand for domestic travel and government initiatives to improve infrastructure. This surge has led to the expansion of Via.com's BAC (Business and Consumer) network to Jaipur, Patna, and other key regional destinations, catering to the rising number of travelers in these areas. As of 2026, over 70% of India's population will have access to online travel booking platforms, further fueling the growth of regional travel.

The expansion of Via.com's network is a strategic move to capitalize on the growing demand for regional travel in India. With over 400 million domestic trips taken in 2025, the market is ripe for disruption. By targeting regional destinations like Jaipur and Patna, Via.com aims to increase its market share and provide travelers with more options for planning their trips. According to industry experts, the regional travel market in India is expected to reach $10 billion by 2028, presenting a significant opportunity for travel companies to expand their services.

The Rise of Regional Travel in India

The growth of regional travel in India can be attributed to several factors, including improving infrastructure, increasing disposable incomes, and government initiatives to promote tourism. The Indian government has launched several initiatives to improve the country's transportation network, including the development of new airports, highways, and railways. These initiatives have made it easier for people to travel, contributing to the growth of regional travel.

Additionally, the rise of low-cost carriers and online travel booking platforms has made it more affordable and convenient for people to plan their trips. According to a report by the Indian Ministry of Tourism, the number of domestic tourists in India increased by 12.7% in 2025, with the majority of travelers opting for regional destinations.

Key Drivers of Regional Travel

The growth of regional travel in India is driven by several key factors, including:

  • Increasing demand for domestic travel: The number of domestic tourists in India is expected to reach 2.5 billion by 2028, driving the growth of regional travel.
  • Improving infrastructure: The development of new airports, highways, and railways has made it easier for people to travel, contributing to the growth of regional travel.
  • Government initiatives: The Indian government has launched several initiatives to promote tourism, including the development of new tourist destinations and the improvement of existing infrastructure.
  • Rising disposable incomes: Increasing disposable incomes have made it possible for more people to travel, contributing to the growth of regional travel.

WhatsApp update brings the new profile tab to iOS users

By Marcus J. Holloway Senior Tech Educator
Published: May 05, 2026 Updated: May 05, 2026 6 Min Read

A significant update is rolling out to iOS users of the popular messaging app WhatsApp, one that promises to enhance the way users manage their accounts and express their identities. The update, which is part of WhatsApp version 16.5.10.73, replaces the traditional Settings tab with a new 'You' tab, located in the bottom right corner of the app. This change is more than just a cosmetic overhaul; it marks a fundamental shift in how WhatsApp approaches user profiles and account management.

The implications of this update are multifaceted, affecting not just the aesthetic of the app but also its functionality and user experience. By introducing a dedicated profile tab, WhatsApp is making it easier for users to control their online presence and manage their accounts more efficiently. This development is particularly noteworthy given the growing importance of digital identity and the need for intuitive tools that facilitate self-expression and privacy management.

Key Specs and Features Breakdown

The new 'You' tab opens up a redesigned profile settings page, where users can manage their account details, privacy settings, and other preferences. One of the standout features of this update is the use of the user's profile photo as the icon for the 'You' tab, a design choice that mirrors the approach taken by Instagram and Threads. This visual cue makes it simpler for users to identify which account is currently active, a feature that will become increasingly important as WhatsApp moves towards introducing multi-account support.

Another significant aspect of the update is the refreshed profile page, which now includes a default cover photo at the top of the screen. Although this feature is currently limited to a select group of users, and the ability to change the cover photo is not yet available, it signals a broader effort by WhatsApp to enhance the visual and interactive elements of user profiles. This move aligns with the app's goal of providing a more engaging and personalized experience for its users.

The update is part of a larger strategy by Meta, WhatsApp's parent company, to enhance the app's capabilities and user experience. By testing and gradually rolling out new features, WhatsApp aims to stay competitive in the messaging app market while addressing the evolving needs and preferences of its user base. The introduction of the 'You' tab and the revamped profile settings page is a key step in this process, reflecting WhatsApp's commitment to innovation and user satisfaction.

How It Compares to the Previous Model

In comparison to the previous version of WhatsApp, the new update offers a more streamlined and intuitive approach to account management. The consolidation of profile settings into a single, easily accessible tab simplifies the process of adjusting preferences and managing one's online presence. This change also reflects a shift towards a more user-centric design philosophy, where the needs and convenience of the user are paramount.

However, it's worth noting that the update may take some time to reach all users, given the phased rollout approach adopted by WhatsApp. This strategy allows the company to monitor user feedback, identify potential issues, and make necessary adjustments before the update is widely available. Despite this, the eventual availability of the 'You' tab and revamped profile settings to all iOS users will mark a significant enhancement to the WhatsApp experience.

The comparison with other messaging apps, such as Instagram and Threads, also highlights WhatsApp's efforts to align with industry trends and user expectations. By adopting similar design elements and features, WhatsApp aims to provide a familiar and cohesive experience across different platforms, enhancing its appeal to a broader range of users.

Performance — What Tests and Users Show

Initial tests and user reports suggest that the updated WhatsApp app performs smoothly, with the new 'You' tab and profile settings page integrating seamlessly into the existing interface. The redesign of the profile page, including the addition of a cover photo, has been well-received by users, who appreciate the enhanced visual appeal and customization options.

However, some users have expressed anticipation for the ability to change their cover photo, a feature that is currently not available. This feedback underscores the importance of ongoing development and user engagement, as WhatsApp continues to refine its features and address the evolving needs of its user base. The company's willingness to listen to user feedback and incorporate it into future updates will be crucial in maintaining user satisfaction and loyalty.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the new 'You' tab in WhatsApp?

The 'You' tab is a new feature in WhatsApp that replaces the Settings tab, providing users with a dedicated space to manage their account, control their identity, and express themselves. It includes a redesigned profile settings page and uses the user's profile photo as its icon.

Can I change my cover photo in the updated WhatsApp app?

Currently, the ability to change the cover photo is not available to all users. The feature is being rolled out gradually, and it may take some time before it becomes widely accessible.

Is the WhatsApp update available to all iOS users?

No, the update is being rolled out in phases. While some users may already have access to the new 'You' tab and profile settings, others may need to wait until the update becomes more widely available.

How does the new 'You' tab reflect WhatsApp's strategy for the future?

The introduction of the 'You' tab and the revamped profile settings page reflects WhatsApp's commitment to enhancing user experience, providing more intuitive account management, and moving towards multi-account support. These developments align with the company's broader goals of innovation, user satisfaction, and competitiveness in the messaging app market.

Conclusion

The update to WhatsApp, bringing the new 'You' tab to iOS users, marks a significant step forward in the app's evolution. By streamlining account management, enhancing profile settings, and adopting a more user-centric design approach, WhatsApp is poised to improve the overall user experience and stay competitive in the messaging app landscape. As the update becomes more widely available and additional features are introduced, users can expect a more personalized, engaging, and efficient WhatsApp experience.

Looking ahead, the introduction of multi-account support and further enhancements to user profiles will be critical in shaping the future of WhatsApp. The company's ability to listen to user feedback, address evolving needs, and innovate in response to market trends will be essential in maintaining its position as a leading messaging app. With its commitment to user satisfaction and its ongoing efforts to refine and expand its features, WhatsApp is well-positioned to continue playing a central role in the digital lives of its users.

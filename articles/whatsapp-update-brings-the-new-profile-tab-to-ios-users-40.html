WhatsApp Update Brings the New Profile Tab to iOS Users

After several weeks of beta testing, WhatsApp officially rolled out its redesigned navigation tab to iOS users on March 17, 2026, with version 16.5.10.73 published to the App Store. The change is deceptively simple on the surface — the familiar gear icon in the bottom-right corner of the app has been replaced by your own profile photo — but it signals a much larger strategic shift for Meta's messaging platform.

According to the official App Store release notes, the update describes it this way: "The settings tab is now a profile tab, where you can manage your account, control your identity, and express yourself. This feature is a first step towards introducing multi-account support."

What Actually Changed — The 'You' Tab Explained

The bottom navigation bar in WhatsApp for iOS has five tabs. The rightmost tab — previously a stylized gear icon labeled "Settings" — is now the "You" tab, displaying your profile photo as its icon. Tapping it opens the same account and privacy controls as before: your name, status, profile photo, privacy settings, chats, notifications, linked devices, and storage management.

The settings themselves have not been removed or reorganized in any significant way. The change is primarily a navigation identity shift — framing the tab around the user as a person rather than as a settings panel. The redesigned profile page that opens now places your profile photo and name more prominently at the top, with settings grouped below in a cleaner visual hierarchy.

Version Details

The update is in WhatsApp version 16.5.10.73 (also referenced as version 26.10.73 in some reports, depending on the version numbering format). It is available on the App Store now. Not all users receive it simultaneously — WhatsApp rolls out updates in waves.

Why Your Profile Photo is Now the Icon

The design choice to use your profile photo as the tab icon is not cosmetic — it's functional groundwork for multi-account management. According to WABetaInfo, which first reported the feature in February 2026, the logic mirrors what Instagram and Threads already do: when you're managing multiple accounts on one device, your profile photo in the navigation bar gives you an at-a-glance visual indicator of which account is currently active.

With a gear icon, all accounts look identical in the navigation bar. With your profile photo, switching between a personal account (showing your face) and a work account (showing a logo or different photo) becomes visually immediate. You no longer have to open settings and read your account name to confirm which identity you're operating as.

As 9to5Mac noted: "By using the user's profile photo as the icon for the You tab and highlighting it on the You page itself, WhatsApp makes it easier to identify which account is active at a glance. This is similar to Instagram and Threads."

Multi-Account Support: Now Live on iOS

The bigger news — announced by Meta on March 26, 2026 — is that two-account support is now officially live on iOS. Meta confirmed: "Two accounts, one phone — now on iOS: You can now have two WhatsApp accounts logged in at the same time on iOS — just like on Android. There's no need to carry two phones to keep work and personal chats separate. You'll always know which account you're in because your profile picture will now be visible in the bottom tab."

This is a feature Android users have had for some time. iPhone users who manage separate personal and work phone numbers — or who want to keep family and professional messaging completely separate — can now do so on a single device. The "You" tab redesign is the UI foundation that makes this practical.

The Cover Photo Feature Being Tested

Alongside the tab redesign, WhatsApp is testing a cover photo header at the top of the profile page — a large banner image similar to what Facebook and LinkedIn use. According to WABetaInfo, this feature is currently limited to a small group of users and cannot yet be customized: it displays a default image that users cannot change.

When it rolls out widely and allows user customization, it will give WhatsApp profiles a more complete social-platform feel — a significant visual upgrade from the current simple profile photo and name layout. The direction is clear: Meta wants WhatsApp profiles to feel like owned identity spaces, not just account settings screens.

WhatsApp Plus: The New Paid Tier

Running in parallel with the profile redesign, Meta has begun rolling out WhatsApp Plus — a paid subscription tier — to iOS users. According to WABetaInfo, WhatsApp Plus gives access to premium sticker packs with fullscreen animations, profile customization features, and other personalization tools aimed at heavier users who want to make their WhatsApp experience more expressive.

WhatsApp Plus is distinct from WhatsApp Business — it does not add business-specific features like catalogs or quick replies. It is a personalization layer for regular consumer accounts. Pricing details for the subscription have not been broadly publicized, and the rollout to iOS is gradual.

Meta AI Photo Editing in Chat

Another feature confirmed in Meta's March 26 announcement: Meta AI photo editing is now available inside WhatsApp chats. Before sending a photo, you can use Meta AI to remove distracting objects from the background, replace the background entirely, or apply creative styles — all without leaving the chat. Meta noted availability may vary by region and user.

How to Get the Update — Rollout Status

WhatsApp updates roll out gradually, and not every user receives each feature at the same time. To check if you have the update:

StepAction
1Open the App Store on your iPhone
2Tap your profile icon in the top-right corner
3Scroll down to find WhatsApp and check for a pending update
4If version 16.5.10.73 or higher is shown, tap Update
5After updating, check the bottom-right tab of WhatsApp — if it shows your profile photo instead of a gear, the update is active

Not Seeing the Update Yet?

Multiple users — including tech journalists at 9to5Mac and MacRumors — reported not having the new tab even after updating the app. WhatsApp still controls which users see the new features via server-side flags. If your bottom-right tab still shows a gear, you simply haven't been assigned to the new experience yet. It will come in time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the new 'You' tab in WhatsApp for iOS?

The 'You' tab replaces the traditional Settings gear icon in the bottom-right corner of WhatsApp on iOS. It uses your profile photo as the tab icon and opens a redesigned profile settings page. It was officially rolled out in WhatsApp version 16.5.10.73 on March 17, 2026.

Why did WhatsApp replace the Settings gear with a profile photo?

WhatsApp is preparing for multi-account support. When running two accounts on one phone, your profile photo in the navigation bar gives an instant visual cue about which account is active — an approach borrowed from Instagram and Threads.

Does the 'You' tab still have the same settings?

Yes. All the same settings are present — privacy, notifications, chats, linked devices, storage — just in a redesigned layout with your profile more prominently displayed.

What is the WhatsApp cover photo feature?

WhatsApp is testing a large banner-style cover photo at the top of the profile page, similar to Facebook. Currently it shows a default image that cannot be changed. Customization is expected in a future update.

Is WhatsApp multi-account now live on iOS?

Yes. Meta confirmed on March 26, 2026 that two separate WhatsApp accounts can now be active simultaneously on one iPhone — the same feature Android has had for some time.

What is WhatsApp Plus?

WhatsApp Plus is a new paid subscription tier offering premium sticker packs and profile customization features. It is separate from WhatsApp Business and is aimed at regular users who want more personalization options.

Conclusion

The shift from a gear icon to your own face in WhatsApp's bottom navigation bar is a small visual change with significant strategic implications. It is the clearest signal yet that Meta is ready to bring full multi-account support to WhatsApp on iOS — a feature that millions of users who juggle personal and professional numbers have been waiting years for.

Combined with the cover photo test, WhatsApp Plus subscription, and Meta AI photo editing, the March 2026 update cycle represents the most personality-forward set of changes WhatsApp has shipped in years. The messaging app — long criticized for feeling utilitarian compared to Instagram — is clearly trying to grow into a platform where users feel genuinely expressed, not just connected.

If you haven't seen the new tab yet, keep your app updated and wait. WhatsApp's phased rollout means it reaches everyone eventually — but not on the same day.
